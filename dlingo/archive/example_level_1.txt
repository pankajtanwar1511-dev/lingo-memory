---------------------------------
Level 1-1 [ Order food ]
---------------------------------
すし、ください。
Sushi, please.
おちゃ、ください！
Green tea, please!
ごはんとみず、ください！
Rice and water, please!
みずです。
It's water.



TIP
Welcome to Japanese!
Japanese has three writing systems. In hiragana, which you can use to write most Japanese words, each character represents a 
syllable
, and there are no spaces between words. We use romaji to represent Japanese sounds using the Roman alphabet:
すしとみず

[sushi to mizu]
sushi and water
In Japanese, verbs go at the end:
すしです。

[sushi desu]
It's sushi.


---------------------------------
Level 1-2 [ Describe people ]
---------------------------------
いしゃです。
She's a doctor.
せんせいです。
He's a teacher.
かっこいいべんごしです。
She's a cool lawyer.
やさしいひとです！
He's a nice person!

TIP
A teacher, the teacher
Japanese does not have words like a or the. For example, the word 
せんせい
 [sensei] can mean both teacher and a teacher.


---------------------------------
Level 1-3 [ Introduce yourself ]
---------------------------------
こんにちは、はなです。
Hello, I'm Hana.
	けんです、どうぞよろしく。
	I'm Ken, nice to meet you.
こんばんは。
Good evening.
じゃあね、またあした。
Bye, see you tomorrow.



TIP
R‑E‑S‑P‑E‑C‑T
When you address someone, you use the title 
さん
 [san] after their name to show respect. When you use this with somebody's first name, it doesn't have a translation in English.
けんさん、こんにちは。

[Ken‑san, konnichiwa]
Hello, Ken.
Notice that greetings like hello and bye often come before the name in English, but after it in Japanese.


---------------------------------
Level 1-4 [ Order food and drink ]
---------------------------------
これはカレーです。
This is curry.
それはラーメンです。
That's ramen.
それはおいしいですか。
Is that tasty?
	はい、これはおいしいです。
	Yes, this is tasty.



TIP
On topic
Using 
は
 [wa] after a word makes it the topic of the sentence. The rest of the sentence is a comment about it.
カレーはおいしいです。

[karee wa oishii desu]
Curry is tasty.
The hiragana 
は
 is only pronounced wa as a topic marker. Otherwise, it is pronounced ha.


---------------------------------
Level 1-5 [ Talk about countries ]
---------------------------------
アメリカはおおきいです。
America is big.
カナダじんです。
I'm Canadian.
けんさんはブラジルじんですか。
Is Ken Brazilian?
なおみさんはイギリスじんです。
Naomi is British.



TIP
I'm Japanese!
You’ve seen that hiragana and katakana can be mixed in the same sentence. They can also be mixed within a single word! Compare the examples below:
これはアメリカです。

[kore wa Amerika desu]
This is America.
わたしはアメリカじんです。

[watashi wa Amerikajin desu]
I'm American.


---------------------------------
Level 1-6 [ Ask for directions ]
---------------------------------

ホテルはどこですか。
Where is the hotel?
	ホテルはそこです。
	The hotel is there.
バスていはここですか。
Is the bus stop here?
	はい、ここです。
	Yes, it's here.
これはデパートですか。
Is this a department store?
なおみさんはどこですか。
Where is Naomi?



TIP
Where are we?
In Japanese, most question words come right before 
ですか
 [desu ka]. Compare the following questions:
ホテルはここですか。

[hoteru wa koko desu ka]
Is the hotel here?
ホテルはどこですか。

[hoteru wa doko desu ka]
Where's the hotel?
Remember that words like hotel in Japanese can be translated as hotel, a hotel, or the hotel depending on the context!

---------------------------------
Level 1-7 [ Describe belongings ]
---------------------------------
これはわたしのぼうしです。
This is my hat.
わたしのかさはあかいです。
My umbrella is red.
わたしのくつはどこですか。
Where is my shoe?
これらはわたしのコートです。
These are my coats.



TIP
My word!
To say something belongs to you, use the phrase 
わたしの
 [watashi no]:
これはわたしのかさです。

[kore wa watashi no kasa desu]
This is my umbrella.


---------------------------------
Level 1-8 [ Talk about neighbors ]
---------------------------------
たなかさんはあかるいひとです。
Mr. Tanaka is a cheerful person.
なかやまさんはかっこいいです。
Ms. Nakayama is cool.
やまぐちなおみさんはおもしろいです。
Naomi Yamaguchi is funny.
なかやまはなさんはやさしいですか。
Is Hana Nakayama nice?



TIP
Won't you be my neighbor?
You’ve seen 
さん
 [san] used after a first name to show respect when addressing or describing someone else. It can also be used after a last name and translated as Mr. or Ms.
たなかさんはやさしいです。

[Tanaka‑san wa yasashii desu]
Mr./Ms. Tanaka is nice.
When using both first and last names in Japanese, the last name comes first.
なかやまはなさんです。

[Nakayama Hana‑san desu]
She's Hana Nakayama.


---------------------------------
Level 1-9 [ Tell time ]
---------------------------------
一じです。
It's one o'clock.
いま二じです。
It's two o'clock now.
すみません、いまなんじですか。
Excuse me, what time is it now?
	三じごろです。
	It's about three o'clock.
	
	
	
TIP
Time to learn kanji!
The kanji writing system is used for most words in Japanese. There are over 2,000 kanji characters.  Kanji can really help you tell words apart! For example, notice the words that sound the same in the following sentences:
たなかさん、こんにちは。

[Tanaka‑san, konnichiwa]
Hello, Mr. Tanaka.
さんじです。

[sanji desu]
It's three o'clock.
Some kanji look like what they mean. See how the kanji for one, two, and three have that many lines:
kanji
hiragana
一
いち
二
に
三
さん
In this course, you’ll slowly learn more and more about kanji.
