# Japanese Learning Tips


## Level 1 Tips

### 1-1 - Order food

Japanese has three writing systems. In hiragana, which you can use to write most Japanese words, each character represents a 
syllable
, and there are no spaces between words. We use romaji to represent Japanese sounds using the Roman alphabet:
すしとみず

### 1-2 - Describe people

Japanese does not have words like a or the. For example, the word 
せんせい
 [sensei] can mean both teacher and a teacher.

### 1-3 - Introduce yourself

**When you address someone, you use the title**

さん
 [san] after their name to show respect. When you use this with somebody's first name, it doesn't have a translation in English.
けんさん、こんにちは。

### 1-4 - Order food and drink

**Using**

は
 [wa] after a word makes it the topic of the sentence. The rest of the sentence is a comment about it.
カレーはおいしいです。

### 1-5 - Talk about countries

You’ve seen that hiragana and katakana can be mixed in the same sentence. They can also be mixed within a single word! Compare the examples below:
これはアメリカです。

### 1-6 - Ask for directions

**In Japanese, most question words come right before**

ですか
 [desu ka]. Compare the following questions:
ホテルはここですか。

### 1-7 - Describe belongings

**To say something belongs to you, use the phrase**

わたしの
 [watashi no]:
これはわたしのかさです。

### 1-8 - Talk about neighbors

**You’ve seen**

さん
 [san] used after a first name to show respect when addressing or describing someone else. It can also be used after a last name and translated as Mr. or Ms.
たなかさんはやさしいです。

### 1-9 - Tell time

The kanji writing system is used for most words in Japanese. There are over 2,000 kanji characters.  Kanji can really help you tell words apart! For example, notice the words that sound the same in the following sentences:
たなかさん、こんにちは。

### 2-11 - Talk about interests

**We’ve seen**

です
 (desu) can mean to be, as in 
学生です
 (gakusei desu). But 
です
 (desu) is used in many other situations that aren’t translatable to English.
For example, to say I like water! or I like tea!, use 
が

好きです
 (suki desu).
水が好きです。

### 2-14 - Use na-adjectives

**それ**

(sore) and

 (are) both mean that, so what’s the difference?

### 2-17 - Describe your family

Japanese has lots of words to talk about family! When you talk to your family members, or when you talk about other people’s family, you generally use the words that end with that respectful 
‑さん
 (san).
おかえりなさい、お父さん。

### 2-18 - Discuss chores

**Remember, when you count**

nouns
, you need to use a 
counter
 with the number. We’ve seen 
人
 in words like 
日本人
 (nihonjin). This kanji means person, and you also use it to count people. 
兄が一人います。

### 2-20 - Discuss a family visit

**In English, we say on Monday. In Japanese, you put**

に
 after days of the week to show when you do something.

### 2-21 - Order pastries

**Use words like**

この
 (kono) before a 
noun
, like This food is great! Use words like 
これ
 (kore) when you don’t mention the noun, like This is great! 
この朝ご飯はおいしいです。

### 2-25 - Get around a theme park

When saying what color something is in Japanese, we need to remember a few things! If the words end in 色 (which actually means color!), like 
オレンジ色
, put a 
の
 between the color and the thing!

### 2-26 - Discuss media

**When you use the word**

あまり
 with 
‑ません
, it shows that you don’t do something very much.  
彼女はあまり走りません。

### 2-27 - Communicate at work

In English, when we talk about how much time something takes, we usually use the words approximately or about before the time. In Japanese, you use the word 
くらい
 after the approximate time!
